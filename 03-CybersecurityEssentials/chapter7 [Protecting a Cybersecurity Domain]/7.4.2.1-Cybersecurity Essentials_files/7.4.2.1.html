Guards and Escorts

All physical access controls including deterrent and detection systems ultimately rely on personnel to intervene and stop the actual attack or intrusion. In highly secure information system facilities, guards control access to the organizationâ€™s sensitive areas. The benefit of using guards is that they can adapt more than automated systems. Guards can learn and distinguish many different conditions and situations and make decisions on the spot. Security guards are the best solution for access control when the situation requires an instantaneous and appropriate response. However, guards are not always the best solution. There are numerous disadvantages to using security guards including cost and the ability to monitor and record high volume traffic. The use of guards also introduces human error to the mix.
