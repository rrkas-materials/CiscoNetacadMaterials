Security Controls
Definition of Security Control Types
Controls that restore resources, functions, and capabilities after a violation of security policy.
Controls that restore the system back to a state of confidentiality, integrity, and availability, and return systems to normal after unauthorized activity occurs.
Controls that discourage cyber criminals from gaining unauthorized access to information systems and/or sensitive data.
Controls that restrict or stop unwanted or unauthorized activity from happening.
Controls that can be very simple, such as a motion detector or security guard. They can also be more complex, such as an intrusion detection system.
Controls that provide options to other controls to bolster enforcement and support of a security policy. It can also be a substitution used in place of control that is not possible under the circumstances.
