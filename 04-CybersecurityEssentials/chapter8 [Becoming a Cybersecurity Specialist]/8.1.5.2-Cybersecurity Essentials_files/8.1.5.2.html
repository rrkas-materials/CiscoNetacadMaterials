Managing Threats to the Public Cloud

Organizations can implement various measures to manage threats to the physical facilities:

  • Multifactor authentication

  • Use of encryption

  • Implement one-time passwords, phone-based authentication, and smartcards

  • Distributing data and applications across multiple zones

  • Data backup procedures

  • Due diligence

  • Security awareness programs

  • Policies

The table shown in the figure matches up Public Cloud Domain threats with the countermeasures used to manage them.

Threat: Data breaches Countermeasures: Multifactor authentication, Use of encryption, and One-time passwords, phone-based authentication, and smartcards Threat: Loss or theft of intellectual property Countermeasures: Due diligence, Use of encryption, and Data backup Threat: Compromised credentials Countermeasures: Multifactor authentication, Use of encryption, and One-time passwords, phone-based authentication, and smartcards Threat: Use of federated identity repositories Countermeasures: Multifactor authentication, Implement one-time passwords, phone-based authentication, and smartcards Threat: Account hijacking Countermeasures: Multifactor authentication and Implement one-time passwords, phone-based authentication, and smartcards Threat: Lack of understanding on the part of the organization Countermeasures: Due diligence on agreement responsibilities Threat: Social engineering attacks that lure the victim Countermeasures: Security awareness programs Threat: Compliance violations Due diligence Countermeasures: Policies
